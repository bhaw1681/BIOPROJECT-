📖 How it Works

1
Load samples — DNA mixed with loading dye is pipetted into wells at the negative (−) electrode end.
2
Apply voltage — Electric current causes negatively charged DNA to migrate toward the positive (+) electrode.
3
Size separation — Smaller fragments move faster through the agarose matrix. Larger fragments are retarded.
4
EtBr staining — Ethidium bromide intercalates DNA and fluoresces under UV light, revealing bright orange bands.
5
Size estimation — Compare band position with the DNA ladder to estimate fragment size in base pairs (bp).
